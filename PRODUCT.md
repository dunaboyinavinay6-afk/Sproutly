# PRODUCT.md — Sproutly

## Register

brand — this is a marketing site for a web development studio. The site itself
is the proof of the work; a visitor's impression is the deliverable.

## Product purpose

Sproutly is a web development studio. It sells three things, framed by
deliverable:

1. Custom web pages — single pages engineered to convert (landing, campaign, microsites).
2. Websites — complete multi-page websites that scale.
3. Custom automation tools — internal software and integrations that remove repetitive work.

The site's job: make a prospective client believe Sproutly builds with real
craft, and get them to start a project conversation.

## Users

- Founders and marketers at small-to-mid businesses who need web work done well
  and fast, and who can tell good craft from a template.
- They arrive evaluating, slightly skeptical (the web is full of identical agency
  sites). They are deciding whether this studio is worth an email.

## Brand voice

Three physical-object words: warm, precise, quick.
- warm — a studio you'd actually want to talk to, not a faceless vendor.
- precise — engineering craft; nothing sloppy.
- quick — they ship; momentum is part of the pitch.

Copy is plain and confident. No buzzwords, no em dashes, no restated headings.

## Aesthetic direction

Lane: bold confident light. Not dark-mode-tech, not editorial-magazine.

- Color strategy: Committed. One deep evergreen carries ~30% of the surface.
  A deep evergreen (not bright SaaS-mint) deliberately avoids the
  "growth brand -> bright green" first-order reflex while honoring the name.
  Warm clay is a minor secondary accent only.
- Theme: light. Scene sentence — a founder comparing three agencies on a
  laptop in a bright office on a weekday morning, deciding who to email.
- Typography: Bricolage Grotesque (display) + Hanken Grotesk (body). Both off
  impeccable's reflex-reject list.
- Imagery: code-drawn UI visuals (browser frames, automation flow), no stock
  photos. Correct for a dev studio per the brand register.

## Anti-references / anti-goals

- No dark background + neon accent + monospace (first-order tech reflex).
- No editorial-magazine layout (display-serif italic, drop caps, ruled columns).
- No identical 3-card service grid with icon tiles above headings.
- No hero-metric template (big number + label + supporting stats).
- No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no side-stripe borders.
- No fabricated client names, logos, or statistics.

## Strategic principles

- The site must look like a sample of Sproutly's work, because it is one.
- Asymmetry and editorial weight over centered-stack templates.
- One emotional beat per fold.
